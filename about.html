About

Anjuman Islam

Welcome to the Barberton Anjuman Islam Masjid website. The name "Anjuman Islam" refers to an Islamic association or society, reflecting the community-led nature of this masjid. Situated in the heart of Barberton, the masjid serves as a spiritual and communal hub for local Muslims — offering the five daily salaah (prayers), Jumu’ah services, and various Islamic educational activities. With a history dating back to the late 1800s, the masjid stands as a symbol of unity, heritage, and devotion.

Our History

Serving the Muslim community of Barberton since the late 1800s

A Timeline of Growth
  • Late 1800s – Muslims settled in De Kaap Valley
  • 1927 – First Maktab established, led by Hf. Chohan
  • 1953 – Land donated by Suleiman Ebrahim Bhushkaw
  • 1954 – Present masjid construction begins
  • 1983 – Major renovations undertaken
  • 2002 & 2016 – Minor restorations
Key Community Activities
  • Nikah & Janazah
  • Madressa & Hifz
  • Adult Ta’lim
  • Zakaat & Lillah initiatives
  • Dawah & Tabligh
  • Rural madressa support
  • Community outreach
  • Inmate Ramadan support
  • Basic ladies facilities
  • Ulema collaboration

The Barberton Anjuman Islam Masjid remains a beacon of faith, service, and unity — continuing the legacy of its founding community.
